Captain Smart has finally broken his silence

Captain Smart has finally broken his silence on his suspension from Angel FM as the morning show host. In an interview with Paul Adom Otchere on the Good Evening Ghana show on Thursday, May 6, 2021, Captain Smart explained what led to his suspension. He revealed that the owner of the station, Dr Kwaku Oteng, said in a conversation that he had been told that he (Captain) had insulted President Nana Akufo-Addo, his vice, Dr Mahamudu Bawumia, and Alan Kyerematen.

UGC He told Oteng that the information was false and even declared his intention to apologise on-air if evidence was brought that he (Captain) called those people fools as being alleged. READ ALSO Angel FM General Manager confirms suspension of Captain Smart; names 4 people he offended in new video Notwithstanding his explanation to his boss, Captain Smart said he was asked to step aside after the day's show. According to him, Oteng intimated that there was pressure on him from the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) and other government agencies because of the supposed insults. "He (Oteng) thus told me to stay away until such a time the pressure will cool off," Captain Smart said. Earlier, Captain Smart had indicated that he was not afraid to lose his job and would have gladly left if he had been told to resign. "If he had called to tell me, Captain, I cannot work with you again. I would have had no problem because I will not go hungry. My brain works," he said. Reports surfaced on social media on Thursday, May 6, 2021, that Captain Smart had been taken off the airwaves. The reports were started by a post on the Facebook page of IMANI President, Franklin Cudjoe. Cudjoe had indicated that his sources had told him Captain Smart had been taken off air for overly criticising President Nana Akufo-Addo's government.

Umat invent new technology to clean polluted water bodies...

The Vice Chancellor of the University of Mines and Technology(UMaT),Prof.Richard Akwasi Amankwa and his team today, made a presentation to the Ministry.

The presentation was on a technology that has been developed to clean water bodies polluted by the activities of illegal miners(galamsay).

The technology is capable of causing particles that pollute water bodies to settle within a period of one month.

The Deputy Ministers-Designate, noted that, the newly developed technology will help in sanitizing our polluted water bodies and therefore, called for collaboration to ensure its success. They also reiterated Government's determination to combat the illegal mining menace and the need to engage all relevant stakeholders such as the water Resource Commission on this technology to ensure it works. present at the meeting were the Deputy Ministers-designate, Hon. Benito Owusu Bio and Hon. George Mireku Duker including some Senior Officials of the ministery. Also present were representatives from other stakeholders including MESTI, Ministry of Infomation, Minerals Commission, Water Resources Commission, Ghana National Association of Small Scale Miners and the Media.
